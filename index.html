Plant Care Guide

Learn optimal watering practices for different types of plants

Vegetable Watering Guide

Most vegetables need 1-1.5 inches of water per week, either from rainfall or irrigation.

  • Root vegetables (carrots, beets): Water deeply 1-2 times per week
  • Leafy greens (lettuce, spinach): More frequent but lighter watering
  • Fruiting plants (tomatoes, peppers): Deep watering, allowing soil to dry between waterings

Our smart sensors can automatically detect moisture levels and provide the perfect amount of water based on vegetable type.

Best Watering Practices

  • Water early morning to reduce evaporation

  • Use drip irrigation to target plant roots directly

  • Mulch around plants to retain moisture

  • Adjust watering based on rainfall and temperature

Flower Watering Guide

Different flower varieties have varying water needs based on their type and growth stage.

  • Perennials: Once established, deep watering 1-2 times weekly
  • Annuals: More frequent watering, especially in hot weather
  • Drought-tolerant flowers: Minimal watering once established

Our smart irrigation system automatically adjusts to ensure optimal moisture levels for beautiful blooms all season.

Flower Watering Tips

  • Water at the base to avoid wetting foliage and blooms

  • Group plants with similar water needs together

  • Newly planted flowers need more frequent watering

  • Consider soil type when determining watering frequency

Trees & Shrubs Guide

Proper watering is critical for establishing and maintaining healthy trees and shrubs.

  • New plantings: Regular deep watering for first 2 years
  • Established trees: Deep watering during drought periods
  • Evergreens vs. deciduous: Different seasonal needs

Our smart system can be set up with deep-root watering protocols specifically designed for trees and shrubs.

Tree Watering Best Practices

  • Water slowly and deeply to encourage deep root growth

  • Create a watering ring or basin around new trees

  • Use soaker hoses or drip systems around the drip line

  • Reduce frequency but increase volume as trees mature

Lawn Care Guide

Keeping your lawn healthy requires understanding watering needs based on grass type, season, and climate.

  • Cool-season grasses: More water in spring and fall
  • Warm-season grasses: Highest needs during summer growth
  • Establishment: New lawns need frequent, light watering

Our smart irrigation system uses soil sensors to determine exactly when your lawn needs water, preventing both over and under-watering.

Lawn Watering Tips

  • Water deeply (1-1.5 inches) but infrequently to promote deep roots

  • Early morning watering (4-10 AM) is most efficient

  • Adjust mowing height seasonally to retain moisture

  • Reduce watering during dormant periods
